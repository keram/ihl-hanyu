语法一 | yǔfǎ yī | grammar 1

The interrogative pronoun "怎么"

The structure "怎么+ verb/adjective" is used to ask about the reason for something, indicating surprise or astonishment.
For example:

Subject   Predicate (verb/adjective)
  怎么 动词/形容词
你 怎么 不高兴？
今天 怎么 这么热？
今天你们 怎么 都没去打蓝球？

语法二 | yǔfǎ èr | grammar 2

Reduplication of measure words

When a measure word is reduplicated, it means "every/each",
emphasizing that a specific feature is shared by every member in a
certain group, usually followed by "都".

For example:

Subject AA 都 dōu
同学们 个个 都很高兴。
这个商店的衣服 件件 都很漂亮。
这个星期（我） 天天 都吃样肉。

语法一 | yǔfǎ yī | grammar 1

The modal verb "还|hái"

It indicates the continuation of an action or a state.
Its negative form is "还没|hái méi".

For example:

  1. 八点了，他还在睡觉。
  2. 你怎么还没吃饭？
  3. 他还在教室学习呢。

The adverb of time "就|jiù"

It is used for emphasis, indicating that the speaker thinks something happened early or went fast and well.

For example:

Subject 就 | jiù

我 坐飞机一个半小时就到北京了。
（我） 一刻钟就到了。

语法二 | yǔfǎ èr | grammar 2

The modal particle "呢"

It is used at the end of a declarative sentence or after a sentence
with an adjectival or verbal predicate to confirm a fact and convince
someone in an exaggerative mood.

For example:
－ 八点上课，的间还早呢。
－ 医院离我们这儿还远呢。
－ 坐公共汽车要一个多小时呢！

语法 | yǔfǎ | grammar

The interrogative sentence "…, 好吗？"

It is used to ask about another person‘s idea or opinion.
It indicates the distance from a place, moment or purpose.

For example:

  1. 我们一起去吃饭，好吗？
  2. 你明天下午给我打电话，好吗？
  3. 我们下午去看电影，好吗？

The adverb "再"

It indicates the repetition or continuation of an action or a state.
It can also indicate that an action will happen under a certain circumstance.

For example:

    Predicate
Subject 再 Verb (object)
你 再 看看这本书吧。
你 再 给我打电话吧。
（你） （让我想想）再 告诉你。

Pivotal sentence

The predicate of a pivotal sentence is made up of two verbal phrases,
the object of the first verb being the subject of the second.
The first verb is often a causative verb,
such as "请" (to invite), "让" (to let) and "叫" (to ask).

For example:

Subject Verb Object/Subject Predicate
我 请 你 吃饭。
你 让 我 再想想。
我 叫 人 去看看。

Reduplication of verbs

The reduplicative form of a verb indicates a short time,
a small quantity, a slight degree or an attempt, conveying a relaxed and
casual mood. It is often used in spoken Chinese.

For example:

Table 1: Reduplicative forms of disyllabic verbs.
A AA
学习 学习学习
准备 准备准备
运动 运动运动
Table 2: Reduplicative forms of monosyllabic verbs.
A AA A-A
听 听听 听－听
说 说说 说－说
看 看看 看－看

语法 | yǔfǎ | grammar

The preposition "从"

It introduces the starting point of a period of time,
a distance, a process or a sequence, often used together with "到".

For example:

从 A 到 B …
从 北京 到 上海 要坐几个小时飞机？
从 老人 到 孩子 都喜欢吃菜果。
从   下个星期一   开始上班。

Indicating order "第~"

"第“ is often used before a numeral-measure-word phrase to indicate order.

For example:

第 数词 量词 名词
第 一 本 书
第 二 个/份 工作
第 一 次 跳舞
